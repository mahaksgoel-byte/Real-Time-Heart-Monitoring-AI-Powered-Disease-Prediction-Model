Personalized Prevention Plans

Explore tailored prevention plans based on your cardiovascular risk level. ❤️

0–20% Low Risk 🌟

Suitable for: BMI < 25, BP < 130/80, non-smokers, active lifestyle, normal cholesterol & glucose.

Precautions 🛡️

  • Maintain regular exercise (e.g., brisk walking 30 min/day). 🏃
  • Eat a Mediterranean or DASH-style diet. 🥗
  • Annual health screenings (BP, cholesterol, glucose). 🩺
  • Avoid starting smoking or alcohol abuse. 🚭
  • Maintain healthy weight and stress levels. 😊

Risks if Ignored ⚠️

  • Slow buildup of atherosclerosis.
  • Early rise in BP or LDL over years.
  • Missed opportunity for early lifestyle correction.

21–40% Mild Risk 🌱

Suitable for: Slightly elevated BP (130–139/80–89), borderline cholesterol (LDL 130–159), overweight.

Precautions 🛡️

  • Moderate lifestyle changes (reduce sodium, increase fiber, BMI < 25). 🥕
  • Cut down processed foods, fried items, and sugars. 🍔❌
  • Regular physical activity + aerobic exercise (150 min/week). 🚴
  • Manage screen time and sleep (≥7 hrs/day). 😴
  • Track BP and lipids every 6–12 months. 📊

Risks if Ignored ⚠️

  • Hypertension Stage 1, LDL elevation.
  • Early vascular inflammation or endothelial dysfunction.
  • Gradual transition to metabolic syndrome.

41–60% Moderate Risk ⚠️

Suitable for: BP > 140/90, BMI > 28, glucose pre-diabetic, LDL > 160, sedentary lifestyle.

Precautions 🛡️

  • Cardiologist or physician consultation advised. 👩‍⚕️
  • Possible medication (e.g., low-dose statins or ACE inhibitors). 💊
  • Supervised exercise plan. 🏋️
  • Intensive dietary intervention (low sodium, plant-based). 🥦
  • Weight loss target: 5–10% of current weight. ⚖️

Risks if Ignored ⚠️

  • High risk of angina, TIA, or silent ischemia.
  • Organ-level changes (e.g., left ventricular hypertrophy).
  • Microvascular complications (kidney, retina).

61–80% High Risk 🚨

Suitable for: Multiple comorbidities (e.g., hypertension + diabetes), BMI > 32, poor activity, smoking history.

Precautions 🛡️

  • Intensive pharmacological treatment (statins, antihypertensives). 💉
  • Cardiac workup (ECG, stress test, echocardiography). 🩺
  • Tobacco/alcohol cessation programs. 🚬❌
  • Tight glycemic and BP control. 📉
  • Consider cardiac rehabilitation. 🏥

Risks if Ignored ⚠️

  • Coronary artery disease, stroke, or cardiac ischemia.
  • Heart failure or arrhythmias.
  • Progressive kidney and vascular damage.

81–100% Very High Risk 🆘

Suitable for: Age > 65 with hypertension, obesity (BMI > 35), high cholesterol & glucose, high resting HR, poor lifestyle.

Precautions 🛡️

  • Emergency medical evaluation and intervention. 🚑
  • Invasive diagnostics (angiography, CT scan, etc.). 🩻
  • Medications: statins, beta-blockers, anticoagulants. 💊
  • Frequent monitoring: BP, ECG, lipids every 1–3 months. 📅
  • Dietary consult + supervised rehab. 🥗🏋️

Risks if Ignored ⚠️

  • High risk of myocardial infarction, stroke.
  • Multi-organ failure or systemic complications.
  • Fatal arrhythmias or thrombotic events.
